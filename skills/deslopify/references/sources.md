# Sources

Where the catalog comes from, what each source contributes, and how it may be reused. Tells and patterns are paraphrased in this repo, never copied verbatim.

## Primary

- **[tropes.fyi](https://tropes.fyi)** — Ossama ([ossama.is](https://ossama.is)). The base catalog this whole project is built on: word choice, sentence structure, tone, formatting, and composition tropes with examples. If you read one source, read this one.

## Catalog extensions

- **[Wikipedia: Signs of AI writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing)** — WikiProject AI Cleanup. The broadest editorial catalog anywhere: era-stratified vocabulary, copulative avoidance, formatting and citation forensics, chatbot residue strings, and — just as useful — a list of *ineffective* indicators that anchor the anti-overcorrection section. License: CC BY-SA 4.0 (paraphrased here, with credit).
- **[stop-slop](https://github.com/hardikpandya/stop-slop)** — Hardik Pandya. A skill with the same goal; contributed false agency, vague declaratives, lazy extremes, and the metronomic-rhythm rules. License: MIT.
- **[slop-forensics](https://github.com/sam-paech/slop-forensics) / [EQ-Bench slop score](https://eqbench.com/slop-score.html)** — Sam Paech. Statistically derived slop words, bigrams, and trigrams from large LLM story corpora; source of the creative-prose slop section. Academic write-up: [Antislop (arXiv:2510.15061)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.15061). License: MIT.
- **[Kobak et al., "Delving into LLM-assisted writing in biomedical publications through excess vocabulary"](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adt3813)** (Science Advances, 2025; [preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.07016)). Empirical excess-vocabulary methodology over 15M PubMed abstracts; source of the academic excess-word list. License: CC BY.
- **[Shaib et al., "Measuring AI 'Slop' in Text"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.19163)**. Expert-derived taxonomy framing slop as low information density and relevance, not just banned words; source of the content-density section.
- **[Pangram Labs: Most common AI phrases](https://www.pangram.com/resources/most-common-ai-phrases)**. The largest empirically derived flat phrase list, from millions of human-vs-AI documents. Cited for corroboration; their pages are proprietary.
- **[Sean Kernan, "13 Signs You Used ChatGPT To Write That"](https://seanjkernan.substack.com/p/13-signs-you-used-chatgpt-to-write)**. Reader-perspective tells: uniform sentence length, colon-titles, immediate bullets, hedging clusters, too-perfect surface.
- **Paul Graham** — the April 2024 "delve" post and later remarks on AI-written founder emails reading like polished journalism in the wrong register. Source of the register-mismatch tell.
- **[awesome-slop](https://github.com/hwajongpark/awesome-slop)** — Hwajong Park. Curated index of slop tools, papers, and wordlists, including non-English slop patterns. Good discovery feed for future catalog updates. License: CC0.

## Related work

- **[skill-deslop](https://github.com/stephenturner/skill-deslop)** — Stephen Turner ([write-up](https://blog.stephenturner.us/p/deslop)). A prior Claude skill merging tropes.fyi and stop-slop with a scoring rubric. Built independently of this one; worth a look if you want a score-based approach.
